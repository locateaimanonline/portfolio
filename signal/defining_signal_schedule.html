Signal Policy Wizard | Defining Policy Schedules

Ensure you have Manage Signal Policies permissions. See General Permissions. Review Signal Policy Wizard | Configure Policy Settings to complete configuring Signal policy settings before continuing with this task.
This task represents the fifth and final step in the five-step process of configuring a Signal policy. In this step, you define when the policy is active and how often its actions are allowed to execute.

The scheduling phase in the SOTI MobiControl Signal policy wizard allows administrators to control the policyâ€™s operating window. While scheduling is optional, it provides important safeguards against unnecessary monitoring, alert fatigue, and unintended automation.

Define Signal policy schedules to:

  • Control when policies run and prevent execution during off-hours or maintenance windows.
  • Reduce unnecessary alerts by limiting monitoring to relevant time frames.
  • Align policy execution with business hours and operational needs.
For an overview of all five steps in the Signal policy workflow, see Signal Policy Wizard Overview.
  1. Choose a Schedule Type to define when the policy is active.
    • Always: The policy is active immediately after you save it and continues running with no defined end time.
    • Custom: Define specific days and times (in UTC) when the policy is active. You can also configure activation and deactivation dates.
  2. When defining a custom schedule:
    1. define the days of the week when the policy should be active in the Enable Policy On field.
    2. Define the daily time range during which the policy is active by setting the start and end times (in UTC) that apply to the selected days in the Between fields.
  3. Expand Advanced Settings to specify policy activation time and deactivation times.
    1. Use Activation Date and Activation Time to control when the policy begins running.
    2. Enable Set Deactivation Date and define when the policy should stop by specifying a Deactivation Date and Deactivation Time to deactivate the policy.
  4. Enable Limit Action Execution to prevent repeated action triggers.

    When enabled, actions execute only when the policy condition status changes. This is useful to prevent unnecessarily repetitive triggers.

    Note: This option is available only for conditions related to Signal properties.
    Attention: This feature is incompatible with Device Side Evaluation. If both options are enabled, an error message appears. See Signal Policy Wizard | Configure Policy Settings.
  5. Select Finish to save your Signal policy.
You have successfully completed the five-step Signal policy configuration wizard. If no schedule restrictions are defined, the policy activates immediately.
